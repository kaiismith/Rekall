# Overview

> **The workspace where every call is remembered.**

Rekall is a self-hostable call intelligence platform — a place for teams to meet, talk, and later look back at what was said, decided, and agreed. It sits in the space between a video-conferencing tool and a shared organizational memory: meetings happen *inside* Rekall, and the knowledge from them stays there.

---

## Why Rekall exists

Most meetings evaporate the moment they end. Notes get scattered across chats, decisions get lost, and action items quietly go unowned. Teams repeat the same conversations because nobody can find what was said three weeks ago.

Rekall is built on a simple premise:

> **If the meeting happened in Rekall, the team shouldn't have to remember it — Rekall already does.**

That means real-time conversation *and* durable recall, in one place, owned by your organization.

---

## What Rekall replaces

Teams today juggle half a dozen tools to get through a single meeting and its follow-through:

| Today | With Rekall |
|---|---|
| Zoom / Meet / Teams for the call | Built-in video room |
| Separate chat app for side conversation | In-room chat that persists |
| Google / Outlook calendar for scheduling | Native calendar *(roadmap)* |
| Manual note-taking | AI Notes *(roadmap)* |
| Otter / Fireflies for transcripts | Native ASR *(roadmap)* |
| Notion / Confluence to write up the outcome | AI Ask over meeting memory *(roadmap)* |
| Email threads to follow up | Action items tied to the call *(roadmap)* |
| Nothing at all for meeting analytics | Org + department dashboards *(roadmap)* |

One workspace, one source of truth, one login.

---

## Who Rekall is for

- **Small to mid-sized teams** who want a self-hosted, privacy-respecting alternative to "Zoom + Notion + Otter" stitched together.
- **Engineering-led companies** that prefer to own their data and run their own infrastructure.
- **Distributed teams** that need meetings to leave a trail behind them — decisions, action items, agreements.
- **Organizations in regulated spaces** (healthcare, legal, finance) where meeting recordings and transcripts must stay inside the org's perimeter.

If you're looking for a video-call tool that's *just* a video-call tool, Rekall is probably overkill. If you're looking for the memory layer of your organization, Rekall is being built for you.

---

## What makes Rekall different

**Self-hostable by design.** Your calls, your transcripts, your data — on infrastructure you control. No third-party processors, no "enterprise plan" required.

**Built to be remembered, not just held.** Other tools treat the recording as an afterthought. Rekall treats the conversation itself as the product — everything in the room is captured, organized, and made retrievable.

**One workspace, not a stack.** Organizations, departments, meetings, call history, and (soon) knowledge retrieval all live in the same place, under the same account model.

**Honest about what it is.** Rekall is in active early development. The meeting and organization primitives are live today; the recall layer is what comes next.
